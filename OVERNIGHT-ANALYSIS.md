# GREENLIGHT Mid-Session Analysis — Rounds 5–12

**Date:** 2026-02-16  
**Analyst:** Bishop  
**Scope:** Rounds 5 through 12 (Round 8 was a failed browser test, so effectively rounds 5–7, 9–12)

## 1. Systems Added & How They Interact

Over 8 productive rounds, GREENLIGHT went from a basic card game to a layered system-of-systems:

| Round | System | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | **Tag/Keyword System** (Momentum, Precision, Chaos, Heart, Spectacle) | Cross-talent synergies, build archetypes |
| 5 | **Tag-Aware Script Abilities** | Scripts reward specific tag concentrations |
| 6 | **Juice Pass** (~15 animations, narrative text, tier reveals) | Game feel |
| 7 | **Content Expansion** (+7 talent, +4 scripts, +8 chemistry pairs) | Replayability |
| 7 | **NG+ / Director Mode** | Endgame difficulty scaling |
| 9 | **Procedural Sound Effects** | Audio feedback |
| 9 | **Combo Counter** | Synergy streak visualization |
| 10 | **Archetype Focus** (50%+ tag concentration → quality bonus) | Specialization reward |
| 10 | **Encore** (push-your-luck at wrap) | Risk/reward tension |
| 10 | **Legacy Perks** (10 meta-progression unlocks) | Cross-run motivation |
| 11 | **Legacy Perks Wired In** (8/10 actually functional) | Fixing round 10's broken promises |
| 12 | **Onboarding System** (auto-tutorial, phase tips, help button) | New player experience |

**Interaction map:**  
Tags → Feed Archetype Focus → Influence casting decisions → Affect Encore risk calculus → Contribute to Legacy perk unlocks → Shape future runs.

This is a solid loop on paper. The concern is how many of these layers a player actually *perceives* vs. how many are invisible plumbing.

## 2. Balance Concerns — "Always Correct" Strategies

### 🔴 Chaos Build Looks Dominant

The Chaos archetype has a troubling risk/reward profile:
- Incidents carry Chaos tags, fueling Oliver Cross and Jimmy Chang cards
- Nightmare Alley script gives +1 quality per Chaos tag
- Savage Lands script makes each Incident give +$1M budget back
- Roxanne Blaze's incidents generate 2 Chaos tags each
- Chaos Embracer legacy perk makes first incident give +3 quality

**The problem:** Chaos builds turn the game's primary punishment mechanism (incidents) into fuel. A well-constructed Chaos deck *wants* incidents, which means the core tension of "incidents are bad" — the emotional engine of production — evaporates. When the "risky" strategy also has the highest ceiling AND recovers economically via Savage Lands, it's not really risky anymore.

### 🟡 Archetype Focus Bonus May Be Too Generous

+9 quality at 80%+ focus is enormous. In a game where the difference between HIT and BLOCKBUSTER might be 15-20 quality points, a single passive bonus providing +9 is a huge chunk. This creates a meta where **mono-tag builds are almost always better than hybrid builds**, which reduces the interesting casting decisions the tag system was supposed to create.

### 🟡 Encore Is Probably +EV Too Often

With full deck information visible (remaining incidents / remaining cards), a player can calculate exact risk. If the deck has 8 cards left and 1 incident, that's 87.5% chance of +3 bonus on an action card. The rational play is almost always to Encore unless you're at 2 incidents with a thick remaining deck. This makes it feel like a "tax" you always pay rather than a genuine decision.

### 🟢 Economy Seems OK

The $1 hiring discount from Talent Whisperer and $3M starting budget from Veteran Producer seem modest enough to not break the early game.

## 3. Feature Bloat Assessment

**Honest verdict: Yes, it's getting bloated.**

The game has accumulated 8 major systems in 8 rounds. Let's sort by necessity:

### Keep (Core Identity)
- **Tag system** — This IS the game's strategic identity. Non-negotiable.
- **Draw-2-pick-1 with block** — The core mechanic. Great.
- **Chemistry pairs** — Flavor + reward for exploration.
- **Sound + animations** — The juice is excellent and makes everything feel good.
- **Onboarding** — Essential if anyone besides us will ever play this.

### Questionable (Could Simplify)
- **Archetype Focus** — Good idea but the bonus scaling could be folded into script abilities instead of being a separate system. Do we need *both* tag-aware scripts AND a generic focus bonus?
- **Encore** — If it's almost always correct to take it (see above), it's just an extra click. Either make it riskier or cut it.
- **NG+ / Director Mode** — Simple multiplier modes. Fine but not exciting. These are the "we ran out of ideas" version of difficulty.

### Consider Cutting
- **Legacy Perks** — 10 perks, 2 still broken, complex tracking. The *idea* is good but the execution is half-baked. 8/10 working after a dedicated fix round is a code smell. Consider reducing to 4-5 perks that are simple and impactful, or deferring the entire system until the core game is locked.
- **Combo Counter** — Pure cosmetic but adds visual noise. The sound escalation alone might be enough without the text overlay.

### The Real Issue: Vertical vs. Horizontal

Rounds 5-12 added features **horizontally** — more systems, more content, more UI. What's missing is **vertical depth** in the existing systems. The core draw-2-pick-1 loop hasn't evolved since round 1. There are 41 talent but many still feel interchangeable within their tag archetype. 12 scripts but the abilities are mostly "+N per tag" variants.

**The game is wide but not deep.**

## 4. What the Next 3 Rounds Should Focus On

### Round 13: Balance & Pruning
- **Nerf Chaos builds** — Incidents should never be purely upside. Consider: Chaos tags give quality but incidents still cost budget, or cap Chaos tag bonuses.
- **Flatten Archetype Focus curve** — Change from +2/+4/+6/+9 to +1/+2/+4/+6. Or remove it entirely and let script abilities handle tag rewards.
- **Make Encore riskier** — Hide the exact remaining card count. Or make Encore draw from a *separate* penalty pool so you can't just count cards.
- **Cut or simplify Legacy Perks to 5** — Remove the 2 that don't work, combine similar ones.

### Round 14: Depth, Not Breadth
- **Rival Studios / Competition** — This has been on the "next round" list since round 10. It would add the one thing the game is truly missing: *external pressure*. Right now you're playing solitaire. A rival studio competing for box office share would make market timing and genre selection matter more.
- **Distinct script abilities** — Move away from "+N per tag" toward abilities that change *how you play production*. Example: a script that lets you draw 3 pick 1 but adds an incident to the deck. Or a script where you play cards face-down and reveal at wrap.

### Round 15: Playtesting With Humans
- **Actually get someone else to play it.** Round 8 tried browser testing and failed. Round 12 added onboarding. It's time to validate whether all these systems make sense to someone who didn't build them.
- Record a playthrough, note where confusion happens.
- Simplify based on real feedback, not theory.

## 5. Fun Factor Assessment

**Rating: 7/10 — Impressive but exhausting.**

**What's fun:**
- The sound and animation work is genuinely delightful. Card slams, combo escalations, and tier reveals all feel great.
- Draw-2-pick-1 with tag implications creates real "hmm" moments.
- Chemistry pairs are a fun discovery mechanic.
- The theming is charming — "making movies" is a great wrapper for deckbuilding.

**What's not fun:**
- **Decision fatigue.** By mid-production you're tracking: quality target, incident count, tag counts, archetype focus %, clean wrap status, potential synergies, encore probability. That's too many variables for a game that should be snappy.
- **The meta-game is confusing.** Legacy perks, NG+ modes, career stats, unlock tracking — it's a lot of infrastructure for a game that hasn't proven its core loop is fun enough to replay 10+ times.
- **No narrative arc within a run.** Despite the "make movies" theme, each season feels identical: greenlight → cast → produce → release. There's no escalation, no story beats, no "season 4 is when things get real" moment. The difficulty just comes from higher targets.
- **The Neow screen is a Slay the Spire copy** that doesn't earn its place. In StS, Neow rewards are meaningful because the entire game tree branches from them. Here, the choices feel arbitrary.

**The core question:** Is this a game someone would choose to play for 30 minutes, or is it an impressive tech demo? Right now it's closer to the latter. The *craft* is excellent — the sound system, the animations, the data modeling. But the *game design* is lagging behind the engineering. More systems won't fix that. Fewer, sharper systems might.

---

## Summary

GREENLIGHT has impressive technical polish and creative theming, but it's at an inflection point. Eight rounds of additive development have created a system-rich game that risks collapsing under its own weight. The Chaos archetype is probably overpowered, Archetype Focus and Encore may not be creating the decisions they intend to, and the legacy system is half-working. The next rounds should resist adding features and instead prune, balance, and — critically — get human eyes on it.

The game needs an editor more than it needs a writer.
